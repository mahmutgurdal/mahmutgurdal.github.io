About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Human Development and Family Science at Virginia Tech, where I study the complex interplay between children, families, and technology in the earliest years of life.

My research centers on understanding how individual characteristics — such as child temperament, developmental stage, and parental attitudes — and broader environmental factors shape patterns of media use in early childhood. I draw on developmental psychology, family systems theory, and media studies to approach these questions with rigor and nuance.

I am particularly interested in how screen time recommendations can be better tailored to the heterogeneous realities of families, and in developing evidence-based guidance for parents and practitioners navigating an increasingly digital world.

Research Interests

  • Early childhood media use
  • Individual & environmental differences
  • Parental mediation strategies
  • Child temperament & development
  • Digital technology & families
  • Screen time guidelines

Research Projects

🧒

Child Temperament & Media Use

Examining how individual child characteristics — including activity level, emotional reactivity, and attention — relate to differential patterns of screen media engagement in children ages 0–5.

🏠

Home Media Environment

Investigating how household media ecology — including device availability, parental modeling, and family rules — shapes the quantity and quality of young children's media experiences.

👨‍👩‍👧

Parental Mediation Strategies

Studying the range of strategies parents use to manage and co-engage with media, and how these strategies vary by parent beliefs, child characteristics, and sociodemographic factors.

📊

Evidence-Based Guidance

Translating empirical findings into actionable, differentiated recommendations for parents, pediatricians, and early childhood educators navigating questions of appropriate digital media use.
